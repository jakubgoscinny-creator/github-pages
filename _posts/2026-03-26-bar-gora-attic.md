date: 2026-03-26
---

The upper floor of our place at **Bar Góra** is an A-frame attic — sloped walls at 60°, a 293 cm peak, and two rooms to work with. It's currently empty. The plan is to turn the big room into a proper chill-out space.

## The Space

- **Big room:** 292 × 347 cm, peak 293 cm, 60° wall angle
- **Small room (by the stairs):** ~225 × 118 cm

No furniture yet. The sloped walls make standard furniture mostly useless, which is actually a gift — it forces a more creative approach.

## Shared Element: Wooden Chest (~350 zł)

Both concepts include a **solid pine chest (~120×60×60 cm)** for storing blankets and spare bedding. Best source: Allegro, around 350 zł. Looks great against white walls and doubles as a bench.

---

## Concept A — Zen Zone (~1 800 zł)

The big room becomes a **floor-level lounge**. No legs, no frames, nothing that fights with the sloped ceiling.

**The centerpiece:** a custom-cut **polyurethane foam mat (T35 density) at 292×347×20 cm**, ordered to size from Allegro (~450 zł). Covered with two elastic fitted sheets (200×200 cm each) — quick, clean, easy to wash.

**On top of that:**
- 6× large floor cushions 70×70 cm (bawełna/cotton, ~80 zł each = ~480 zł)
- 3× back-support floor cushions 80×65 cm against the walls (~90 zł each = ~270 zł)
- 2× bolster pillows 60×22 cm for arm/neck support (~55 zł each = ~110 zł)

The vibe is **Japanese-minimalist**. You come up here, take your shoes off, and sink into the floor. No assembly required — just unroll and arrange.

---

## Concept B — Hammocks & Brazilian Chair (~1 900 zł)

The A-frame structure becomes an asset instead of an obstacle. Screw **anchor bolts (M10) directly into the wooden rafters** and hang from them.

**Big room — two Brazilian woven hammocks (~130 zł each = ~260 zł):**
Mounted at ~1.4 m height, using the 60° angled walls as natural anchoring points. You'll need M10 eye bolts (8 zł/ea) screwed min. 60 mm deep into the rafters + carabiner clips. Allegro has complete 2-hook kits for ~60–100 zł.

**Small room — hanging chair (~320 zł):**
A woven Brazilian sling chair on a **360° swivel ceiling hook** (55–65 zł) screwed into the ridge beam. This room becomes a reading nook that gently rotates.

**Floor cushions between hammocks:**
10× floor cushions 65×65 cm in olive/green tones (~70 zł each = ~700 zł) for lying, stacking, sitting cross-legged.

**Mounting note:** The hammock anchors *must* go into the rafters, not the sheathing. Rafters are spaced ~63 cm apart — locate them before drilling anything.

---

## Which One?

**Concept A** wins on simplicity and comfort — it's basically impossible to do wrong and the mat is genuinely luxurious. No drilling, no load calculations.

**Concept B** is more dramatic and uses the A-frame architecture properly. The hammocks look incredible in a space like this. But it requires careful mounting — getting the anchor placement wrong isn't an option at 150 kg load capacity.

Leaning toward B, installing the hammocks first, and treating the floor cushions as phase two.
